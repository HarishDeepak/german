# B1 Exam Information

## Which exam should you take?

### Goethe-Zertifikat B1
- Provider: Goethe-Institut
- Recognized by: German employers, Einbürgerung (naturalisation), visa authorities
- Fee: ~€175–250 depending on location
- Website: goethe.de/prüfungen

### telc Deutsch B1
- Provider: telc GmbH
- Also widely recognized, often slightly cheaper
- Same CEFR B1 standard as Goethe
- Website: telc.net

### ÖSD Zertifikat B1
- Provider: Österreichisches Sprachdiplom Deutsch
- Austrian origin, fully recognized in DE/AT/CH
- Website: osd.at

### DTZ — Deutsch-Test für Zuwanderer
- Provider: telc or Goethe-Institut
- Required for the German integration course (Integrationskurs)
- Covers A2–B1 range, simpler format
- If you are on an Integrationskurs, this may be your required exam

**Recommendation:** Goethe B1 or telc B1 — both fully recognized. Check which is available closest to you and which date suits.

## Goethe-Zertifikat B1 — Full format

### Module 1: Lesen (Reading) — 65 minutes
| Part | Task | Items |
|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Read a longer text, answer 5 multiple-choice questions | 5 |
| 2 | Read 4 short texts (ads, notices), match to 5 people's needs | 5 |
| 3 | Read a text with 10 gaps, choose correct word | 10 |
| 4 | Read a longer text, answer 5 T/F questions | 5 |
| 5 | Read a text, answer 5 questions about it | 5 |

### Module 2: Hören (Listening) — ~40 minutes
| Part | Task | Items |
|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Listen to 5 short audio clips, multiple choice | 5 |
| 2 | Listen to 5 telephone messages, note key info | 5 |
| 3 | Listen to a longer audio, answer 5 multiple choice | 5 |
| 4 | Listen to a discussion, 10 statements — who said it? | 10 |

All audio is played twice.

### Module 3: Schreiben (Writing) — 60 minutes
| Part | Task | Words |
|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Write an email/letter in response to a prompt (formal or informal) | ~100 words |
| 2 | Write a short informal message (note/text) to a friend | ~40 words |

### Module 4: Sprechen (Speaking) — ~15 minutes (with a partner)
| Part | Task |
|------|------|
| 1 | Introduce yourself (name, where from, job, hobbies) |
| 2 | Discuss a topic with your partner and give your opinion |
| 3 | Plan something together and reach an agreement |

---

## Scoring

- Each module is worth 100 points
- Pass mark: **60 points per module** (you must pass ALL four)
- Overall: 60% minimum
- Certificate awarded if you pass all four modules

If you fail one module, you can **retake only that module** (within 2 years in some providers).

## Preparation timeline

### 12+ weeks before exam
- Identify weakest modules (take a practice test to find out)
- Do at least one `/b1-prep` per week per module
- Build vocabulary with `/vocab` 3–4 times per week
- Fix grammar errors with `/grammar-drill` 2–3 times per week

### 6–8 weeks before exam
- Full practice exams once per week (all 4 modules)
- Focus writing: write 2 texts per week minimum
- Focus speaking: daily or every other day
- Error log review every Friday

### 2–4 weeks before exam
- Daily practice (shorter, targeted)
- Simulate exam conditions (timed, no help)
- Review and memorize key phrase lists
- No new grammar topics — consolidate what you know

### Final week
- Light practice only. One practice test, then rest.
- Review phrase lists and connector words
- Confidence building — `/speak` with open conversations
- Sleep and eat well. Exam performance is partly physical.

## Key phrases for the speaking exam

### Self-introduction (Part 1 — 2 minutes)
- Ich heiße [Name] und komme aus [Land/Stadt].
- Ich bin [Alter] Jahre alt.
- Ich arbeite als [Beruf] / Ich studiere [Fach].
- In meiner Freizeit… / Meine Hobbys sind…
- Ich lerne Deutsch, weil…
- Ich wohne seit [Zeit] in Deutschland / in [Stadt].

### Giving your opinion (Part 2)
Starter phrases to memorise:
- Meiner Meinung nach ist es wichtig, dass…
- Ich finde, dass… / Ich denke, dass…
- Ein wichtiger Aspekt ist, dass…
- Einerseits… andererseits…
- Ich bin dafür / dagegen, weil…

### Planning together (Part 3)
- Was hältst du davon, wenn wir…?
- Ich würde vorschlagen, dass wir…
- Das klingt gut, aber vielleicht könnten wir auch…
- Einverstanden! / Ja, das machen wir so.
- Wir könnten uns auf [Vorschlag] einigen.
