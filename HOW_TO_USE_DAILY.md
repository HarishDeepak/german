# How to Use This System Daily

Your practical guide. Read this once, then bookmark it.

---

## Starting a session

Open Claude Code in `D:\German`. That's it. Claude reads `CLAUDE.md` automatically and knows who you are and what you need.

To start practicing, just type one of these:

| What you want to do | What to type |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Speak German, role-play | `/speak` |
| Drill one grammar topic | `/grammar-drill` |
| Learn and test vocabulary | `/vocab` |
| Listening practice | `/listen` |
| Writing task + correction | `/write` |
| Review and drill my errors | `/errors` |
| B1 exam practice | `/b1-prep` |
| Plan this week | `/weekly-plan` |

You can also just say what you want in plain English:
- "I want to practice at the doctor" → runs a speaking scenario
- "Test me on Dativ" → grammar drill on Dativ
- "I only have 10 minutes" → Claude adapts

## The 5-day minimum routine (30 min/day)

| Day | Command | What happens |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| **Monday** | `/speak` | Role-play a real-life scenario in German |

## If you only have 10–15 minutes

Type this at the start: **"Ich habe 10 Minuten."**

Claude will run:
1. One focused drill (speaking OR vocab OR grammar — you pick or Claude picks)
2. Quick wrap-up with one thing to remember

Even 10 minutes of deliberate practice 5x/week beats a 2-hour session once a week.

## How to get speaking practice

**Within Claude Code (text-based speaking):**
Type `/speak` and do the role-play in writing. Write your German responses as if speaking. Claude plays the other person and corrects you.

This builds: vocabulary recall, grammar under pressure, natural phrasing, confidence.

**For real voice practice (recommended alongside this):**
- **ChatGPT Voice Mode**: Best for German conversation practice with voice feedback. Tell it "Wir sprechen auf Deutsch. Korrigiere meine Fehler kurz nach jedem Satz."
- **Gemini Live**: Free, works in German. Good for casual conversation.
- **claude.ai (web app)**: Has voice mode — same quality as this system but with audio.
- **Tandem / HelloTalk**: Real human language exchange partners. Essential for authentic practice.

Use this system for structured learning. Use voice AI or a human partner for free-flowing speaking practice.

## How to upload and use your grammar book

If you have a grammar book PDF (e.g. Schritte Plus, Begegnungen, Menschen, Studio d, Aspekte):

1. Tell Claude: "Ich habe [Buchname] als PDF."
2. Either:
   - Paste specific pages/exercises as text for Claude to work with
   - Describe which chapter you're on: "I'm on chapter 5, Dativ prepositions"
3. Claude will create exercises, drills, and explanations based on the book's structure.

You can also just type: "Das Thema in meinem Buch ist heute: Relativsätze" and Claude will run a targeted session without needing the book itself.

## How to track progress

### Weekly (Friday or weekend)
1. Run `/errors review` — see what's still in your error log
2. Add new errors that came up this week
3. Mark fixed errors as done
4. Update `logs/session_log.md` with what you did this week

### Monthly
1. Run `/weekly-plan review`
2. Look back at `logs/session_log.md` — what did you actually practice vs. plan?
3. Set one goal for next month

### Before the exam
1. Run `/b1-prep` once a week (full module simulation)
2. Keep your error log clean — fix everything before exam day
3. Two weeks before: daily short practice, no new topics

## Error tracking (important — don't skip this)

After every session where you make a mistake, Claude adds it to `logs/error_log.md`.

Check it once a week. Ask yourself:
- Is the same error appearing every week?
- Have I drilled it specifically or just hoped it would go away?

**The most common mistake learners make:** noticing an error, understanding the correction, and then never drilling it. Understanding and producing are different skills. Drill it.

## Content to consume outside sessions

### Listening (do this in your free time)
- **Slow German** (slowgerman.com) — clear, graded podcasts. Start here.
- **DW Nicos Weg** (dw.com) — free A1–B1 video series
- **Deutsche Welle langsam gesprochene Nachrichten** — slow news, daily
- **Tatort / Dark** — German TV. Use German subtitles, not English.

### Reading
- **Dino lernt Deutsch** (Andre Klein) — graded readers for A2 level. Cheap, funny, highly recommended.
- **DW news articles (easy German)** — short, clear texts
- **Reddit r/Germany in German** — real-world written German

### Speaking (real humans)
- **Tandem app** — free language exchange. You help them with English, they help you with German.
- **HelloTalk** — similar, messaging-based
- **italki** — paid tutors, 30-min community tutor sessions are affordable
