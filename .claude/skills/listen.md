# Skill: listen
**Use this skill for:** listening comprehension practice, training your ear, preparing for B1 listening tasks, learning to understand German at real speed.

---

## What this skill does
Since Claude cannot play audio directly, this skill works with: transcripts I paste in, listening exercises Claude creates for me, or guided listening tasks using real resources I go to independently.

## How to activate
- `/listen` — Claude runs a listening comprehension exercise using a transcript or text
- `/listen transcript` — I paste a German transcript and Claude creates comprehension tasks
- `/listen slow` — simplified German, spoken-style text for slower processing practice
- `/listen B1` — B1 exam-style listening task simulation
- `/listen resources` — Claude recommends what to listen to at my level

---

## Session structure (text-based simulation)

### Mode A: Dictation drill
Claude gives me a German sentence (as if I heard it). I type what I understood / would transcribe.
Good for: processing speed, sound-to-text mapping.

### Mode B: Comprehension task
Claude gives me a written text that mimics spoken German (contractions, natural speech patterns). I read once, fast. Then Claude asks:
- 3–5 comprehension questions (B1 style)
- 1 vocabulary question
- 1 follow-up discussion question

### Mode C: Transcript work
I paste a transcript from something I just listened to (podcast, news, video). Claude:
1. Checks my comprehension with questions
2. Explains difficult phrases
3. Pulls 5–8 vocabulary items to learn
4. Notes any grammar structures worth studying

### Mode D: Resource + task
Claude assigns me a specific resource (with link) + listening task. I go listen, come back, report what I understood.

## Recommended listening resources (A2 → B1)

### A2 level (start here)
- **Slow German mit Annik Rubens** — clear, slow podcasts on everyday topics. Free. slowgerman.com
- **Deutsch warum nicht?** — Deutsche Welle radio course, graded levels. dw.com
- **Nicos Weg (DW)** — video series A1–B1, very accessible. dw.com/nicoswege

- **Deutschpodcast** — intermediate conversations, moderate speed
- **Coffee Break German** — structured episodes, excellent for A2–B1

## Listening strategies (B1 level)

1. **Top-down first**: Before listening, look at the task/questions. Predict what you'll hear.
2. **Don't translate word-by-word**: Listen for the overall message first.
3. **Keywords**: Catch the key nouns and verbs. Ignore small words on first pass.
4. **Use context**: If you miss a word, use what comes before and after.
5. **Second listen**: Go back for details and specific answers.

---

## Spoken German features to practise recognising
- **Contracted forms**: *ist es* → *isses*, *haben wir* → *hammwa*, *ich bin* → *ich bin* (often reduced)
- **Dropped letters**: *mal* instead of *einmal*, *ne* instead of *eine*, *n* instead of *einen*
- **Regional accents**: Bavarian, Berlinerisch, Schweizerdeutsch — note differences exist
- **Filler words**: *ähm, also, na ja, halt, eigentlich, sozusagen, quasi*
- **Particle words**: *doch, ja, mal, eben, halt, schon* — these change tone, not literal meaning

## B1 listening task types (exam format)
1. **Short announcements / radio clips** — answer true/false or multiple choice
2. **Conversations between two people** — identify attitudes, problems, decisions
3. **Longer monologue/interview** — identify main points and details
4. **Telephone messages / voicemails** — extract key information

### Typical question types:
- Was ist das Hauptthema?
- Was denkt Person X über...?
- Welche Information ist richtig?
- Was wird vereinbart?
- Welches Problem wird erwähnt?
