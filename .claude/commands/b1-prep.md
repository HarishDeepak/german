**Use this skill for:** B1 exam simulation, exam task practice, exam strategy, timed practice tests, understanding the exam format.

---

## What this skill does
Simulates full or partial B1 exam tasks across all four skills (Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking). Runs timed practice. Gives exam-level feedback. Builds exam confidence.

## B1 Exam overview (Goethe-Zertifikat B1)

### Structure
| Module | Time | Tasks |
|--------|------|-------|
| Lesen (Reading) | 65 min | 5 parts, 30 items |
| Hören (Listening) | 40 min | 4 parts, 30 items |
| Schreiben (Writing) | 60 min | 2 tasks (letter + note) |
| Sprechen (Speaking) | ~15 min | 3 tasks with a partner or examiner |

**Total: ~3 hours. Pass mark: 60% overall (min 60% per module to pass each separately)**

---

## Lesen (Reading) — 5 task types

### Part 1: Longer text + multiple choice
Read an article (≈350 words). Answer 5 questions.
Strategy: Read questions FIRST. Scan for keywords. Answers are in order.

### Part 2: Correspondence (email/letter)
Read a message. Identify the correct response from options.

### Part 3: Classified ads / short texts
Match 5 people's needs to 8 short texts.

### Part 4: Grammar focus (missing words)
Fill in 10 gaps in a short text. Options given (multiple choice).

### Part 5: Notice / sign matching
Match 5 notices to 8 short descriptions.

---

## Hören (Listening) — 4 task types

### Part 1: Short announcements (radio/PA)
5 short clips. Multiple choice or T/F.

### Part 2: Telephone messages / voicemails
5 messages. Note down or choose correct information.

### Part 3: Radio interview / conversation
Longer conversation. 5 questions, multiple choice.

### Part 4: Discussion
Two people discuss. 10 statements: who says what or T/F.

---

## Schreiben (Writing) — 2 tasks

### Task 1: Formal or semi-formal email/letter (≈100 words)
You receive a letter/email and must reply. Must address all bullet points given.

### Task 2: Short informal message (≈40 words)
Quick note to a friend/colleague. Must include 3 content points.

**Marking criteria:**
- Task completion (did you address all points?)
- Vocabulary and grammar range and accuracy
- Coherence and register

---

## Sprechen (Speaking) — 3 tasks

### Task 1: Einen Vorschlag machen und darauf reagieren
With a partner (or examiner playing partner): suggest something, react, plan together.
Example: "Plan an outing together"

### Task 2: Über ein Thema sprechen (Meinung äußern)
Present your opinion on a topic for ~2 minutes. Then discuss with partner.
Topics: environment, technology, health, work, family, travel, money

### Task 3: Gemeinsam etwas planen
Plan something together: a party, a trip, a project. Must reach an agreement.

## B1 Speaking phrases (exam toolkit)

**Starting your turn:**
- Ich würde gern über [Thema] sprechen.
- Meiner Meinung nach ist es wichtig, dass…
- Ich finde dieses Thema sehr interessant, weil…

**Giving an opinion:**
- Ich denke / glaube / meine, dass…
- Ich bin der Meinung, dass…
- Meiner Ansicht nach…

**Agreeing with your partner:**
- Da stimme ich dir völlig zu.
- Das sehe ich genauso.
- Du hast recht, das ist ein guter Punkt.

**Disagreeing politely:**
- Das sehe ich ein bisschen anders.
- Ich bin da anderer Meinung.
- Das stimmt zwar, aber ich denke…

**Making a suggestion:**
- Wie wäre es, wenn wir…?
- Wir könnten doch…
- Was hältst du davon, wenn wir…?

**Reacting to suggestions:**
- Das klingt gut! / Das finde ich toll.
- Hmm, ich bin nicht sicher, ob das eine gute Idee ist.
- Vielleicht wäre es besser, wenn…

**Reaching agreement:**
- Also, einigen wir uns darauf, dass…
- Sind wir uns einig?
- Gut, dann machen wir das so.

**Buying time:**
- Das ist eine interessante Frage…
- Moment, ich überlege kurz.
- Also, ähm… wie soll ich das sagen?

## Practice exam session format

### When I say `/b1-prep`
Claude runs ONE exam task with these steps:
1. Introduce the task format (30 sec)
2. Give me the prompt / text
3. Set a timer suggestion (I manage it)
4. I complete the task
5. Claude gives detailed feedback with:
   - Score estimate (e.g., 7/10)
   - What I did well (specific)
   - What to improve (specific with examples)
   - A model answer / improved version
