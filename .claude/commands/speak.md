# Skill: speak
**Use this skill for:** speaking practice, role-plays, pronunciation feedback, confidence-building, B1 speaking tasks, spontaneous conversation.

---

## What this skill does
Runs structured, interactive speaking sessions tailored to A2→B1. It forces me to produce German first, then corrects and improves my output. Sessions are short, focused, and end with a clear summary of what I did well and what to fix.

## How to activate
Say `/speak` followed by an optional topic or scenario. Examples:
- `/speak` — Claude picks a scenario based on my weak spots
- `/speak Arzttermin` — role-play a doctor's appointment
- `/speak freie Konversation` — open conversation practice
- `/speak B1 Prüfung` — simulate a B1 speaking exam task

---

## Session structure

### 1. Warm-up (1–2 min)
Claude asks 2–3 simple personal questions in German. I answer in German. No correction yet — just activation.

Example warm-up questions:
- Was hast du heute gemacht?
- Wie war dein Tag?
- Was planst du dieses Wochenende?

### 2. Core scenario (5–20 min depending on time)
Claude sets a realistic scene. I play my role. Claude plays the other person AND the tutor simultaneously.

**After each exchange:**
- Claude responds naturally IN the role-play
- Then drops out to correct errors (using the error format from CLAUDE.md)
- Then re-enters the role-play

### 3. Vocabulary rescue (during session)
If I get stuck on a word, I can say "Wie sagt man…?" or "ich weiß nicht das Wort" and Claude gives me the German word + uses it in the sentence I needed.

### 4. Wrap-up (2 min)
At the end, Claude gives:
- 3 things I did well (specific, not generic)
- 3 things to fix (specific errors or gaps)
- 2–3 key phrases from the session to remember

## Scenario bank (rotate based on gaps)

### Daily life
- At the Supermarkt: buying groceries, asking for something you can't find
- At the Bäckerei: ordering, paying, smalltalk
- At the Bahnhof: buying a ticket, asking about delays
- At the Arzt: making an appointment, describing symptoms
- At the Behörde/Amt: asking about forms, making an appointment
- Calling a Vermieter: asking about a flat
- At a restaurant: ordering, complaining politely, paying

### Social
- Meeting someone new at a language exchange
- Talking about your job, hobbies, hometown
- Making plans with a friend
- Disagreeing politely with someone's opinion

### B1 exam speaking tasks
- Task 1: Describe a picture (Bild beschreiben)
- Task 2: Present your opinion on a topic (Meinung äußern)
- Task 3: Plan something together with a partner (gemeinsam planen)
- Task 4: React to your partner's statement

---

## Correction rules for this skill
- Correct grammar errors always, but briefly
- Correct unnatural phrasing (say "a German would say X instead")
- Do NOT interrupt mid-sentence — wait until the turn is over
- Pronunciation: flag if I describe a word and it's likely wrong (note: cannot hear audio, work from typed phonetics or descriptions)

## Key speaking phrases (A2→B1 toolkit)

**Buying time:**
- Moment mal, ich überlege kurz.
- Wie sagt man das auf Deutsch?
- Ich meine… / Also…

**Opinions:**
- Ich finde, dass…
- Meiner Meinung nach…
- Ich bin der Meinung, dass…
- Das stimmt, aber…
- Einerseits… andererseits…

**Agreeing / Disagreeing:**
- Da haben Sie recht. / Du hast recht.
- Das sehe ich anders.
- Ich bin damit nicht ganz einverstanden.
- Das kann ich gut verstehen.

**Making requests politely:**
- Könnten Sie bitte…?
- Entschuldigung, darf ich fragen…?
- Wäre es möglich, dass…?

**Clarifying:**
- Was meinen Sie genau?
- Könnten Sie das wiederholen?
- Ich habe das nicht ganz verstanden.
